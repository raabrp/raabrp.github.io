The Differential Calculus

Typically, when we must to symbolically calculate derivatives, rely on a set of canonical functions from which the target function may be composed or with which it might be approximated. Pedagogically, it is standard to

  • Prove the form of the derivative for an arbitrary polynomial.
  • Prove geometrically the slope of trigonometric functions.
    • (before redefining them in terms of Taylor approximations).
  • Define the transcendental number eee via the solution to f(x)=f′(x)f(x) = f^{\prime}(x)f(x)=f′(x) .
    • (before redefining it in terms of another Taylor approximation).

Usually, at this point, a nice segue into complex numbers is warranted, but the abstract algebraic implications of playing with new fields and types numbers is sadly, seldom revisited by most engineering concerns.
