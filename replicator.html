The replicator equation describes the time-evolution of a probability distribution, with semantics motivated by biological populations in which competing strategies reproduce or replicate. Within a population, the change in the relative frequency πa\pi_{a}πa​ of agents with type a∈Aa \in \mathcal{A}a∈A is expressed in proportion to the current frequency πa\pi_{a}πa​ and the relative fitness rar_{a}ra​ of the type.
