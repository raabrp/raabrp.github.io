About Me#

Hello! My name is Reilly Raab. I’m currently a PhD student in Computer Science at UC Santa Cruz.

I hail from a small town in the foothills of northern California, completed my undergrad (BSc) in physics1 at UC Santa Barbara’s College of Creative Studies, then spent a few years traveling while reading, pursuing a few personal projects, and working in software development.

My current reseach interests are focused on prediction markets and algorithmic game theory, seeking intersections with information theory and “artificial” intelligence.

About this Site#

This a space for me share my interest in various subjects, to publish personal essays, and to communicate about projects or developments which are important to me.

This site is generated using a combination of Javascript and Python. I write content in Markdown2 and publish my changes using git (github).

The code I’ve written for this purpose is published under the MIT license. It is not perfect, but seeks to avoid as much bloat as possible while doing what I want it to. Please, use and hack it to your heart’s content.

This site is currently accessible via:

  • https://reillyraab.com (cloudflare; SSL + caching + DNS A records to github.io IPs)
  • https://raabrp.github.io (natively hosted)
  • http://users.soe.ucsc.edu/~rraab (.htaccess 301 to reillyraab.com)

Privacy#

I store no information about you whatsoever. A single entry in your browser’s localstorage is requested to let your browser remember whether you’ve selected an alternate color scheme for the site, and that’s it.

All network resources (javascript, css, fonts, etc.), including library code, are hosted by this site itself and no content delivery networks are used (except possibly those used by Cloudflare).

Third-Party Privacy#

By using this site, you are an end user of Cloudflare’s services.

Cloudflare acts as a reverse proxy for reillyraab.com and handles HTTPS encryption / decryption with your browser (but not with the origin server at github.io. The lack of end-to-end encryption in this configuration isn’t a huge deal since Cloudflare performs caching and the github.io site is static). For all intents and purposes, when your browser is communicating with this site, it’s communicating with Cloudflare. While HTTPS might hide which specific content you’re viewing on this site from your ISP, this does not hinder Cloudflare from seeing your specific network requests for the content of this site.
